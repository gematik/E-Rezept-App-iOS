The e-prescription app is the official app for e-prescriptions in Germany. gematik publishes the app as the National Agency for Digital Medicine on behalf of the legislature. gematik is also responsible for the data protection of the app.

Further information

Gematik's official information on e-prescriptions in Germany can be found at www.das-e-rezept-fuer-deutschland.de . There you will receive understandable information and answers to frequently asked questions about e-prescriptions.

About this privacy policy

In this data protection declaration we inform you about data processing by the e-prescription app. We strive to provide an understandable presentation of the technical processes. If we haven't been able to do this, please let us know. You can find our contact details under contact person .

This data protection declaration does not apply to the services of other providers who provide the central e-prescription systems. These services include the prescription service, the identity service and the pharmacy directory . Information about the responsible providers of these services can be found under Other responsible parties .

What is this app for?

If you receive an electronic prescription from your doctor, it will be stored encrypted in the prescription service. The prescription service is the central prescription storage in the German health network. The e-prescription app allows you to securely access the prescription service and receive, manage and redeem e-prescriptions.

Is using the app voluntary?

Yes, using the e-prescription app is voluntary. You are also not obliged to provide specific data in the app or to register. However, if you do not log in, you will not be able to use all of the app's functions.

How and why is your data processed?

Set up profiles

You must set up at least one “profile”.

  • The profile data is only saved on your device.

  • The profile name is used for clear display. He is freely selectable.

  • You can set up multiple profiles to manage e-prescriptions for relatives.

Register for the prescription service

Only the patient (or a representative), the prescribing doctor's office and the filling pharmacy may access the data in the prescription service. For every access to your data in the prescription service, your identity must therefore be proven in order to check the access authorization. The identity service is responsible for this identity check . Information about the provider of this service can be found under Other responsible persons .

prove your identity with your electronic health card (eGK) and a suitable app from your health insurance company :

  • When you log in with the eGK, the digital authentication certificate on the chip is read contactlessly (via NFC) and transmitted to the identity service. You give your consent by entering your PIN. The authentication certificate contains your insured person master data (e.g. your name and your insurance number) and is considered electronic proof of identity. Once the identity service has verified the authentication certificate and confirmed your identity, it issues an access key (token) to the e-prescription app. The e-prescription app can use this to log into the prescription service.

  • When you sign up with an app, you must first select your health insurance so that the e-prescription app can connect to the “correct” app. The e-prescription app retrieves the list of health insurance apps available for this purpose from the identity service. After you share your identity data with the e-prescription app via your health insurance app, it forwards the data to the identity service for verification. If your identity can be confirmed, the identity service of the e-prescription app will issue the access key (token) for logging in to the prescription service.

After signing in, your e-prescriptions and dispensing information stored in the prescription service will be downloaded and additionally stored on your device.

If you manage your e-prescriptions via the e-prescription app, your activities will be transferred to the prescription service and implemented there. These activities include, for example, granting access rights and deleting e-prescriptions.

save login data

If you are logged in, you can activate the “Save credentials” function for future logins. The access data - consisting of PIN, CAN, authentication certificate and access key (token) - are then stored in a protected area of your device so that they can be used again. This feature can only be activated if your smartphone has a secure app access protection mechanism. Apple iPhones with Touch ID or Face ID and Android devices with “Strongbox Keymaster” security module currently meet this requirement.

Scan and redeem prescription codes

If you want to save a prescription code and redeem it at a pharmacy via the app, you must first scan it with the camera. Scanning creates an electronic copy of the prescription code. This step takes place exclusively on your device, which means no internet connection is required. You can then redeem the e-prescription at a pharmacy using the app:

  • If you are logged in to the prescription service and assign the e-prescription to a pharmacy for redemption via the app, this will be documented in the prescription service and the prescription status will be changed to “in redemption”. The pharmacy then receives access rights to the e-prescription stored in the prescription service. There is no direct communication between the app and the pharmacy. You can view the current processing status, which is managed by the recipe service, at any time in the app.

  • send the prescription code directly to the pharmacy along with your other order details (e.g. whether you want to pick up the medication or have it delivered) . In this case, the app communicates directly with your pharmacy's communication system. The prescription service is not involved in this communication and is therefore not informed about the order. For your convenience, the transmitted e-prescription will be marked as “sent” in the order overview of your app. The app receives the access data for the pharmacy's communication system from the pharmacy directory.

Note for Android devices:

In order to be able to scan recipe codes error-free even under unfavorable conditions (e.g. poor camera resolution or lighting conditions), the app uses a special interface (Barcode Scanning API from Google ML Kit) with your consent. This interface is part of the Google Play Services, which is already installed on your device. The camera image is analyzed exclusively on your device. However, as with all Android devices with Google Play Services, Google reserves the right to collect certain usage and device data when using the interface (for ML Kit, for example: manufacturer, device model, operating system version, hardware, mobile operator, time zone, language settings , IP address, app version, configuration of ML Kit, number of scans, error messages) within the operating system and transmitted to a Google server for further development of the interface. It cannot be ruled out that this data will be transferred to a third country (e.g. USA), see Who will your data be passed on to ? You can revoke your consent to use the Google ML Kit at any time by deactivating the camera permission of the e-prescription app in the Android settings . Further information can be found in Google's notes on ML Kit https://developers.google.com/ml-kit/vision/barcode-scanning and in the Google data protection declaration.

Pharmacy search

When using the pharmacy search, your search criteria (e.g. addresses or location data) are transmitted to the pharmacy directory . The pharmacy directory then provides the app with a list of pharmacies that match the search criteria.

Note for Android devices:

With your consent, the app uses the map service Google Maps to view the map. The app then transfers your location (if you have approved it) and the locations of the pharmacies found to an interface in your operating system (Google Maps interface). This interface is part of the Google Play Services, which is already installed on your device. Please note the “Note for Android devices” under Scanning and redeeming prescription codes in connection with Google ML KIT, which also applies to the Google Maps interface. When using Google Maps, Google's Terms of Use for Google Maps/Google Earth and the Google Privacy Policy apply .

Notifications to pharmacies

The e-prescription app enables direct encrypted and confidential communication between you and pharmacies.

If you are logged in to the prescription service, communication with the pharmacy takes place via the prescription service, which also archives the messages. The app loads the messages from the recipe service and saves them on your device so that you can see the current status even without registering. Neither the prescription service provider nor gematik have access to the messages exchanged.

If you are not logged into the prescription service, you can use the app to send messages directly to your pharmacy's communication system. The app receives the access data for the communication system from the pharmacy directory. The prescription service is not involved in the communication. Please note: In order to receive messages and view message histories, you must log in to the prescription service.

Digital cost receipts (for privately insured people)

As a privately insured person, you can agree to receive digital cost receipts in the app. Your consent will be noted on the prescription service and can be revoked at any time in the app. If you have agreed, you will only receive cost receipts from pharmacies in digital form via the prescription service. You can use the app to manage your digital cost receipts stored on the prescription service and save them as PDF documents that can be submitted electronically. For easy electronic submission, you can also share the PDF documents directly with your health insurance provider's submission app.

Security features

Connected devices

The “Save credentials” function can be activated on multiple devices. Any device on which you have this feature enabled is called a “connected device”. All connected devices are managed centrally on the identity service. To do this, the e-prescription app transfers device information to the identity service for clear differentiation: manufacturer, device model, device name, operating system version and device name (e.g. “Anna’s iPhone”). The identity service stores this information. To help you keep track, you can see on which devices you have saved your access data under “Connected devices” in the “Settings” area.

If you want to deregister a connected device, you can do so on that device or another connected device. The identity service then deletes the device information and access keys it has stored. The access keys stored on the deregistered device will also be deleted.

Please note: If a connected device model or its operating system version is deemed unsafe, the Identity Service may deregister the device in question. The identity service then deletes the device information and access keys it has stored. . You can then log in to the recipe service again on the deregistered device, provided the security problem has been resolved by an operating system update.

Integrity checks

Smartphones can be operated with a manipulated operating system (so-called “jailbreaking” or “rooting”). Not every affected user is aware that their operating system has been manipulated (e.g. with devices purchased second-hand) and the security risks associated with this. A technical check of the operating system is carried out every time the e-prescription app is started. If evidence of manipulation is detected, a warning is displayed. You can then continue to use the app at your own risk without any restrictions.

For jailbreak detection on Apple devices, the e-prescription app checks the device memory for evidence of apps that can only be installed on jailbroken devices (e.g. Cydia app).

A security function of the operating system is used to detect root on Android devices. To do this, the app generates a random number and information about the e-prescription app (e.g. version) and transfers it to a special interface in your operating system (SafetyNet Attestation API). This interface is part of the Google Play Services, which is already installed on your device (see Scan recipe codes ). The SafetyNet Attestation API then evaluates information about your device and the app that is only accessible to Google Play Services. This evaluation provides conclusions about possible manipulation of the operating system. The result is communicated to the e-prescription app.

App access lock

To make it more difficult for unauthorized access to your e-prescription app, it can be automatically locked every time you close it. Every time it is opened, it must first be unlocked. Depending on what security features your smartphone has, password-based and biometric methods are available.

Please note: If you activate the “Save access data” function, the app lock is also automatically activated.

If you enable password protection, your password will be checked locally during setup to ensure it is sufficiently strong. The password is only saved on your device.

When using a biometric procedure, the security functions of your smartphone are used. The e-prescription app does not receive any biometric data, but only the result of the biometric check by your operating system.

Push notifications

In future versions of the app, you will be able to receive push notifications about communications from pharmacies or new e-prescriptions. To do this, your smartphone registers with the push service of your operating system. The following push services are used: Firebase Cloud Messaging (Android), Apple Push Notification (Apple), Huawei Push Kit (Huawei).

The respective push service provides the e-prescription app with a push identifier. The push identifier is then transmitted from the e-prescription app to the prescription service. If a push message is to be sent to you, the recipe service sends a message with your push identifier to the push service used, which then forwards it to your smartphone. The pharmacy's message remains on the prescription service and is only loaded when you open the e-prescription app.

Usage analysis

If you allow usage analysis, general usage data (e.g. type and extent of use and operation of the app functions) and information about the hardware and software used (e.g. type, version and manufacturer of the device and operating system) as well as selected settings (e.g. language setting) will be collected ) transmitted. The usage data is only evaluated anonymously. You can revoke your consent at any time by deactivating usage analysis in the settings. Your usage data will then no longer be transferred to gematik. You will receive further information before activating usage analysis in the e-prescription app.

Who will your data be shared with?

If you have agreed to the usage analysis, the app will transmit your usage data to gematik's technical service provider for usage analysis (Content Square GmbH) based on your consent.

Otherwise, gematik has no access to the data stored on your device. The app only shares your data with other providers and other third parties in accordance with How and why is your data processed? described scope further.

Neither the e-prescription app nor the providers of the prescription service, the identity service or the pharmacy directory transfer your data to countries where an adequate level of data protection does not exist and European data protection laws may not be enforced (so-called third countries, e.g. USA).

However, we would like to point out that using the e-prescription app generates usage data on your operating system. The manufacturers of the operating systems partially reserve the right to log and evaluate this data. This may also result in device and usage data being transferred to the USA or another third country. In this respect, it is possible that security authorities in a third country access the data transmitted to the manufacturer and evaluate it, for example by linking data with other information about you.

When will your data be deleted?

gematik does not store any personal data from users of the e-prescription app . Therefore, no deletion is necessary or possible on gematik’s part.

You can delete the recipe data, recipe codes, cost receipts and messages stored on your device in the app itself. The e-prescriptions in the prescription service are automatically deleted after 100 days from issue or last change in status (Section 360 Paragraph 11 SGB V). Cost receipts are deleted from the prescription service after 10 years (Section 360 Paragraph 13 SGB V).

Access logs in the recipe service are not stored on your device and cannot be deleted. They are automatically deleted from the prescription service after three years (§ 309 SGB V).

If you delete a profile, all data associated with it, including data downloaded from the recipe service and stored access data, will be deleted. The saved access data will also be deleted if you deactivate “Save access data”.

If you disable push notifications in the e-prescription app, the push identifier stored on your device will be deleted.

Uninstalling the e-prescription app will delete all data stored by the e-prescription app on your device.

Your privacy rights

With regard to the usage data transmitted to gematik with your consent, you have the right to information (Art. 15 GDPR), correction (Art. 16 GDPR), deletion (Art. 17 GDPR), and restriction of processing (Art. 18 GDPR). ) and data portability (Art. 20 GDPR). You also have the right to revoke your consent (see Usage Analysis ). Please note that gematik stores your usage data in anonymized form. It is no longer possible to assign this data to you personally, so that the data protection rights mentioned above no longer apply (Art. 11 Para. 2 GDPR, Section 308 SGB V). You also have the right to complain to a data protection supervisory authority. The data protection supervisory authority responsible for gematik is the Federal Commissioner for Data Protection and Freedom of Information http://www.bfdi.bund.de.
